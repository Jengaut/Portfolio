Petanque court at the water's edge of Verdun

All rights reserved 2022, Jennifer Gauthier

Revenge is a dish best served cold, Petanque!

For sports lovers! No I'm kidding. For those who don't have a sporty gene like me, petanque is a great game to stretch out and enjoy the fresh wind from the water's edge. In petanque, the objective is to score points by placing your balls closer to the goal than your opponent in relation to the jack. You can play three on three, two on two or one on one. You need a set of six balls, a jack smaller than the balls and a measuring cord and you're done.

Warm up your rind at Verdun beach

A little tan and a good beach! Verdun beach is the perfect place to relax and cool off. Wheelchair accessible! Located a stone's throw from the De L'Eglise metro station, behind the Verdun Auditorium, Verdun's urban beach offers unique access to the Saint-Laurent River and its shores.

Palco is my restau!

How about a good little cocktail at the end of the evening! And even if you have a friend who prefers good beer, you will be served with a Quebec microbrewery beer. The Palco is not just a neighborhood bar, but a place of exchange and celebration with a padded decor. On the menu, signature cocktails, a gourmet selection of spirits, privately imported wines and microbrewery beers. On the menu, simple but generous dishes. On the program, events that showcase art and music.
